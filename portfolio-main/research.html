Research

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

My research in Natural Language Processing (NLP) focuses on developing advanced models and algorithms for understanding and generating human language. This includes exploring various NLP tasks such as:

  • Text Preprocessing: Techniques for cleaning and preparing text data for analysis.
  • Sentiment Analysis: Identifying and extracting subjective information from text.
  • Named Entity Recognition: Detecting and classifying proper names in text.
  • Machine Translation: Translating text from one language to another using automated systems.

In my work, I utilize state-of-the-art models such as BERT, GPT, and other transformer-based architectures to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of NLP applications.

Digital Humanities and Contextual Embedding

My research in Digital Humanities integrates computational techniques with the study of human culture and history. A significant focus of my work is on contextual embedding, which involves:

  • Contextual Analysis: Using embeddings to understand the nuanced meanings of words and phrases within specific cultural and historical contexts.
  • Textual Analysis: Applying NLP methods to large corpora of historical texts to uncover patterns and insights.
  • Interdisciplinary Collaboration: Working with scholars from the humanities to apply computational tools to traditional humanities research questions.

By leveraging contextual embeddings, I aim to bridge the gap between quantitative analysis and qualitative interpretation, providing richer and more nuanced insights into humanistic studies.
